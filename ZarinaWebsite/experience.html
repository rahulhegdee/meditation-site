Zarina Parpia has extensive business consulting experience in the private, public, and non-profit sectors. Most recently, she has provided ongoing strategy and operations consulting to the Quixote Foundation in Seattle. She currently teaches business classes at Forest Ridge School High School in Bellevue, WA. Previously, she was an Associate Principal with the Civic Consulting Alliance in Chicago, where she managed a city-wide violence prevention initiative and implemented an overhaul of the City’s grants contracting system. Prior to that, she led projects for Fortune 100 healthcare clients at a management consulting firm in New York and New Jersey. She holds a B.A. from the University of Pennsylvania and an M.B.A. from the Kellogg School of Management at Northwestern University.

Zarina began meditating in 2006 while visiting relatives in India. She has spent almost a year, collectively, in silent retreat. Meditation is one of the most effective methods she has found for creating a freer and more meaningful life.
